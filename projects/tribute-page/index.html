Robert Johnson

The man who sold his soul to the devil and became one of the greatest blues performers of all time.

Who is Robert Johnson?

While very little is known about the man himself, Robert Johnson became a legend. He was rumored to have made a deal with the devil, which gave him, initially a mediocre musician, the ability to play the blues like no one had before. Though his life was cut short, his music lived on to inspire and influence future generations.

Here's a Timeline of Robert Johnson's Life:

  • 1911 - Born in Hazlehurst, Mississippi to Julia Ann Dodds and Noah Johnson, a traveling field hand.
  • 1924 - Attends St. Peters school in Memphis, Tennessee.
  • 1929 - Marries Virginia Travis.
  • 1930 - Loses his wife, Virgina during childbirth.
  • 1931 - Makes his mysterious and infamous trip to the crossroads of Highways 49 and 61 in Clarksdale, Mississippi, where he learns to play guitar from Ike Zimmerman, and is roumored to have sold his soul to the devil in exchange for musical expertise.
  • 1931 - Marries Caletta Craft.
  • 1932 - Leaves his wife, Calletta to become a traveling musician.
  • 1936 - Records his first album, Nothing But the Blues: Crossroad Blues, as well as his first hits, of which he sold 5000 copies. He then meets and begins traveling with Johnny Shines in Hughes, Arkansas.
  • 1938 - Plays a local juke joint called "Three Forks" where he is given a glass of poisoned whiskey. He dies three days later on August 16th at 27 years of age in Greenwood, Mississippi, a victim of the deliberate poisoning. Four months later, the world is informed of Johnson's passing when record producer John Hammond attempts to invite Johnson to play Carnegie Hall.
  • 1961 - Columbia releases an LP, King of the Delta Blues Singers which brings Johnson's music to a worldwide audience.
  • 1980 - Inducted into the The Blues Foundation's Hall of Fame.
  • 1986 - Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 1990 - Columbia releases The Complete Recordings of Robert Johnson, of which, over a million copies are sold, and for which he receies a Grammy for the Best Historical Recording.
  • 2006 - Wins the Lifetime Achievement Award Grammy.
  • 2011 - Is recognized again for the Centennial Celebration of Robert Johnson's 100th birthday.
